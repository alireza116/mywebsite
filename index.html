About Me!

I am a PhD candidate in Computer Science at UNC-Charlotte with a background in Urban Planning, Architecture, and Information Technology. My work is at the intersection of urban studies, computational social science, and design.
My work at UNC-Charlotte includes teaching the introductory computational design course at the School of Architecture. I am also a research assistant and a teaching assistant at the CS department for the Visual Analytics course. Most recently, I have been studying how the flow of information affects the usage of public space and how we can utilize the data to understand the complexity of human activity.
I aim to use these understandings for the betterment of the public domain. I use computational methods to study the flow of people, urban protests, etc. Through my work, I have made various systems including a Visual Analytics system for Urban Planning, one of the first Visual analytics systems for battling misinformation, and I also conduct research on novel ways on battling misinformation on social media.
